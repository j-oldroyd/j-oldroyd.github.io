Let \(x(t)\) denote the amount of salt in the tank after \(t\) minutes, so \(x(0) = 50\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \dv{x}{t} = r_{i}c_{i}-r_{o}c_{o} = 2\cdot1-3\frac{x}{100-t}. \end{equation*}

We can rearrange this to get

\begin{equation*} \dv{x}{t} + \frac{3}{100-t}x = 2. \end{equation*}

This ODE is linear and has integrating factor \((100-t)^{-3}\text{.}\) Multiplying through the ODE by the integrating factor and rewriting it using the product rule then gives us

\begin{equation*} \dv{}{t}[(100-t)^{-3}x] = 2(100-t)^{-3}. \end{equation*}
