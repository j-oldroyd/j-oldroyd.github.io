Item 1.

Find the eigenvalues.

We find the eigenvalues of \(A\) by solving the characteristic equation \(\det(A - \lambda I) = 0\) for \(\lambda\text{.}\) Since \(\det(A - \lambda I) = \lambda^{2}+4\text{,}\) we see that the eigenvalues of \(A\) are \(\lambda_{1} = -2i\) and \(\lambda_{2} = 2i\text{.}\) The fact that these eigenvalues are complex is not a problem. They're still distinct, so our method will work.
