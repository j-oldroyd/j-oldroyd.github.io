Now we need to find \(C_{1}\) and \(k\text{.}\) To find \(C_{1}\text{,}\) we just use the initial condition to get \(C_{1} = 85\text{.}\) The only piece of information that we have left to find \(k\) is the fact that the temperature of the plate drops to \(90\) after \(20\) minutes. In other words, \(T(20) = 90\text{.}\) Therefore

\begin{equation*} 90 = 15+85e^{20k} \end{equation*}

which becomes

\begin{equation*} \ln\frac{75}{85} = 20k\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore \(k = \frac{1}{20}\ln\frac{15}{17}\approx-.006.\)
