Consider the ODE

\begin{equation*} x^{2}y''+xp(x)y'+q(x)y=0. \end{equation*}

Let \(r_{1}\geq r_{2}\) be (real) roots of the indicial equation \(r(r-1)+p(0)r+q(0)=0\text{.}\)

  1. There is a solution of the ODE of the form \(x^{r_{1}}\sum_{n\geq0}^{}c_{n}x^{n}\text{.}\)

  2. If \(r_{1}-r_{2}\) is not equal to an integer, then there exists a second linearly independent solution of the form \(x^{r_{2}}\sum_{n\geq0}^{}d_{n}x^{n}\text{.}\)
