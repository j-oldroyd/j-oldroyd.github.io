To find the solution of the IVP, we just plug in the initial conditions. Since \(y(0) = 1\text{,}\) this gives

\begin{equation*} 1 = c_{1}. \end{equation*}

And since

\begin{equation*} y' = -3c_{1}\sin(3x)+3c_{2}\cos(3x) + \frac{1}{2}\sin(3x)+\frac{3}{2}x\cos(3x), \end{equation*}

\(y'(0)=-1\) gives

\begin{equation*} -1 = 3c_{2}\Rightarrow c_{2} = -\frac{1}{3}. \end{equation*}

So

\begin{equation*} y = \cos(3x)-\frac{1}{3}\sin(3x)+\frac{x}{2}\sin(3x). \end{equation*}
