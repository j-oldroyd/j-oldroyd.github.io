Let \(F_{S}\) denote the force of the spring on the mass. Then by TheoremÂ 2.1.3 we must have \(F_{S} = -kx\) for some \(k>0\text{.}\) Now, by Newton's Second Law we must also have \(F_{S} = 4x''\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} 4x'' = -kx\quad\text{or}\quad4x''+kx = 0. \end{equation*}

So the motion of the mass is modeled by a linear, second-order homogeneous ODE.
