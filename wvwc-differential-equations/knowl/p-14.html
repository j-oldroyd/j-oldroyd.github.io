In Example 1.1.3, \(y = 5e^{x^{2}}\) is not the only solution of \(y' = 2xy\text{.}\) You can check that \(y = 3e^{x^{2}}\) and \(y = -10e^{x^{2}}\) are also solutions. In fact, any function of the form \(Ce^{x^{2}}\) where \(C\) is a constant is a solution of \(y' = 2xy\text{.}\) See Figure 1.1.4.
