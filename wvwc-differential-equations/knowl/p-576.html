\(x(t)\) satisfies the ODE

\begin{equation*} mx''+kx = f(t) \end{equation*}

where \(f(t)\) is the external force. Since the hammer strikes quickly, we can model it as an instantaneous force of the form \(c\delta(t)\text{.}\) And since it provides an impulse of \(3\text{,}\) we can pick \(f(t) = 3\delta(t)\text{.}\) As the mass is initially at rest, \(x\) satisfies the IVP

\begin{equation*} x''+9x = 3\delta(t)\qq{where}x(0) = x'(0) = 0. \end{equation*}

To solve this, we take the Laplace transform of the IVP to get

\begin{equation*} s^{2}X(s)+9X(s) = 3 \end{equation*}

or just

\begin{equation*} X(s) = \frac{3}{s^{2}+9}. \end{equation*}

So \(x(t) = \sin3t\text{.}\)
