Now in addition to the spring force \(F_{S}\text{,}\) we must also worry about the force \(F_{R}\) of the dashpot on the mass. \(F_{R}\) is always going to act against the velocity of the mass, so for simplicity we assume that \(F_{R} = -cx'\) for some \(c > 0\text{.}\) Using \(F_{S} = -kx\) as usual in conjunction with Newton's Second Law gives us the second-order ODE

\begin{equation*} -cx'-kx = mx''\quad\text{or}\quad mx'' + cx + kx = 0. \end{equation*}
