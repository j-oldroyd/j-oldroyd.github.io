Example 6.4.2 Sinusoidal initial temperature

Consider a thin metal bar of length \(\pi\) placed on the \(x\)-axis, with one end at \(x=0\) and the other at \(x=\pi\text{.}\) Assuming that \(c=1\) and that the initial temperature is \(f(x) = 30\sin x\) for \(0\leq x\leq\pi\text{,}\) we can find the temperature distribution using TheoremÂ 6.4.1:

\begin{equation*} u(x,t) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}b_{n}\sin(n x)e^{-n^{2}t}, \end{equation*}

where \(b_{n}\) is the \(n^{\th}\) coefficient of the Fourier series of the odd extension of \(f(x)\text{.}\) The odd extension of \(f(x)\) is \(30\sin x\text{.}\) Furthermore, the Fourier series of \(30\sin x\) is clearly just \(30\sin x\text{.}\) So in other words,

\begin{equation*} b_{n} = \begin{cases} 30 \amp n=1 \\ 0 \amp otherwise.\end{cases} \end{equation*}

Hence

\begin{equation*} u(x,t) = 30e^{-t}\sin x. \end{equation*}
