Item 2.

Find corresponding eigenvectors.

Set \(\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix}v_{1} \\ v_{2}\end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Then \(A\vec{v} = \lambda\vec{v}\) implies that

\begin{align*} v_{1} - 5v_{2} \amp = \lambda v_{1}\\ v_{1} -v_{2} \amp = \lambda v_{2} \end{align*}

or just

\begin{align*} (1-\lambda)v_{1} - 5v_{2} \amp =0\\ v_{1} - (1+\lambda)v_{2} \amp = 0. \end{align*}

Setting \(\lambda=-2i\) in the second equation gives \(v_{1} = (1-2i)v_{2}\text{,}\) so an eigenvector of \(A\) corresponding to \(\lambda_{1} = -2i\) is

\begin{equation*} \vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix}1-2i \\ 1\end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}

Similarly, an eigenvector corresponding to \(\lambda_{2}=2i\) is

\begin{equation*} \vec{v}_{2} = \begin{bmatrix}1+2i\\1\end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
