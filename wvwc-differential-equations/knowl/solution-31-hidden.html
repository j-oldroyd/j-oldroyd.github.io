The ODE that models the motion of this mass is

\begin{equation*} 25x'' + 10x' + 226x = 0, \end{equation*}

and the roots of the corresponding characteristic equation are

\begin{equation*} r_{1} = -\frac{1}{5} \pm \frac{1}{5}\sqrt{-225} = -\frac{1}{5}\pm3i. \end{equation*}

We can already see that the motion must be underdamped since we have complex roots, and the position \(x(t)\) itself is given by

\begin{equation*} x(t) = e^{-\frac{t}{5}}(A\cos3t + B\sin3t). \end{equation*}

Now we can use the initial conditions \(x(0) = -1\) and \(x'(0) = 0\) to find \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\) Doing so, we quickly get \(A = -1\) and \(B = -\frac{1}{15}\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} x(t) = -e^{-\frac{t}{5}}\left(\cos3t + \frac{1}{15}\sin3t\right). \end{equation*}
