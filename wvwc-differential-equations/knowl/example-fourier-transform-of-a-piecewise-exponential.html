Example 7.5.3. Fourier transform of a piecewise exponential.

Let \(f(x) = e^{-2x}\) for \(x>-5\) and \(0\) otherwise. Then the Fourier transform of \(f(x)\) is

\begin{align*} \hat{f}(\omega) \amp = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}f(x)e^{-i\omega x}\,dx \\ \amp = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-5}^{\infty}e^{-2x}e^{-i\omega x}\,dx \\ \amp = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\int_{-5}^{\infty}e^{-(2+i\omega)x}\,dx \\ \amp = -\frac{1}{(2+i\omega)\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-(2+i\omega)x}\Big]_{-5}^{\infty}\\ \amp = \frac{e^{(2+i\omega)5}}{(2+i\omega)\sqrt{2\pi}}. \end{align*}
