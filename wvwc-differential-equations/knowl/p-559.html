To be specific, let \(f(t)\) be a force that acts only from \(t=a\) to \(t=b\) (and is otherwise \(0\)). Then the impulse of the force \(f(t)\) over the interval \([a,b]\) is given by

\begin{equation*} p = \Int{a}{b}f(t)\dd{t}. \end{equation*}

We view the impulse as essentially describing how the force acts over a short time interval, so we can switch from modeling instantaneous forces to instantaneous impulses. And since the impulse is a number, all we really need to do is model an instantaneous unit impulse; any other impulse we can get by multiplication by a constant.
