The temperature is the function \(u(x,t)\) given by

\begin{equation*} u(x,t) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}b_{n}\sin(n x)e^{-n^{2}t}, \end{equation*}

where \(b_{n}\) is the \(n^{\th}\) coefficient of the Fourier series of the odd extension of \(f(x)\text{.}\) The odd extension of \(f(x)\) is \(30\sin x\text{.}\) Furthermore, the Fourier series of \(30\sin x\) is clearly just \(30\sin x\text{.}\)
