We begin by finding the characteristic equation, which is

\begin{equation*} r^{4}-1=0\quad\text{or}\quad (r^{2}-1)(r^{2}+1)=0. \end{equation*}

This has solutions \(r=\pm1,\pm i\text{.}\) This means that \(y_{1} = e^{x},y_{2}=e^{-x},y_{3}=e^{ix}\) and \(y=e^{-ix}\) are all solutions of the ODE. Since the roots of the characteristic equation are all distinct, this means that these solutions are linearly independent from each other. Since we have four linearly independent solutions, we can then construct the general solution of this ODE:

\begin{equation*} y = c_{1}e^{x}+c_{2}e^{-x}+c_{3}e^{ix}+c_{4}e^{-ix}, \end{equation*}

which we can also rewrite using Euler's Formula \(e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta+i\sin\theta\) to get

\begin{equation*} y = c_{1}e^{x}+c_{2}e^{-x}+A\cos x+B\sin x. \end{equation*}
