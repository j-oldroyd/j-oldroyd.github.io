Consider the non-homogeneous linear ODE with constant coefficients given by

\begin{equation} ay'' + by^\prime + cy = f(x).\tag{4} \end{equation}

If \(f(x)\) were zero then we could solve this by finding the roots of the characteristic equations and using them to determine the appropriate form of the solution. Although that's no longer enough if \(f(x)\neq0\text{,}\) our method for solving homogeneous equations still plays an important role.
