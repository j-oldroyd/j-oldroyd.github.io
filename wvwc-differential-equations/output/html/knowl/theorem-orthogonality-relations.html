Theorem 7.1.4. Orthogonality Relations.

Let \(m,n\) be whole numbers with \(m,n>0\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi}\sin mx\cos nx\,dx = 0 \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} \int_{-\pi}^{\pi}\sin mx\sin nx\,dx = \begin{cases} \pi \amp m=n \\ 0 \amp m\neq n\end{cases}\text{ and }\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}\cos mx\cos nx\,dx = \begin{cases} \pi \amp m=n \\ 0 \amp m\neq n\end{cases}\text{.} \end{equation*}
