The first thing we will do is make sure that the ODE actually has a power series solution at \(x=0\text{.}\) To do this, we need to show that \(x=0\) is an ordinary point of the ODE, which requires finding the appropriate \(P(x)\) and \(Q(x)\) from TheoremÂ 5.3.2. If we divide through the ODE by \(x^{2}+2\) we obtain
\begin{equation*} y''+\frac{4x}{x^{2}+2}y^\prime+\frac{2}{x^{2}+2}y = 0\text{,} \end{equation*}
and so
\begin{align*} P(x) \amp= \frac{4x}{x^{2}+2}\\ Q(x) \amp= \frac{2}{x^{2}+2}\text{.} \end{align*}
