One thing we can notice right away about solutions of \(x''+\omega_{0}^{2}x = 0\) is that they should all be periodic (see Definition 7.1.2), which, of course, makes sense!. To see why, note that the roots of the characteristic equation are \(\pm i\omega_{0}\text{,}\) which means that the solutions may be written in the form
\begin{equation*} x = c_{1}e^{-i\omega_{0}t} + c_{2}e^{i\omega_{0}t}, \end{equation*}
which we can rewrite as
\begin{equation*} x = A\cos\omega_{0}t + B\sin\omega_{0}t \end{equation*}
using Euler’s Formula. Hence every solution of \(x''+\omega_{0}^{2}x = 0\) is a sinusoidal wave.
