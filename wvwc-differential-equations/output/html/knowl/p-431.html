The above power series was written in terms of powers of \(x\text{,}\) but this doesn't have to be the case. We can also write power series in terms of powers of \(x-a\text{,}\) where \(a\) is some constant. A power series of the form

\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}c_{k}(x-a)^{k} \end{equation*}

is said to be centered at \(a\). For such a series, the formula for the \(c_{k}\) is given by

\begin{equation*} c_{k} = \frac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!}. \end{equation*}
