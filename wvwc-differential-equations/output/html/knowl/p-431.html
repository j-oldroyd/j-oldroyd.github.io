So we'd like to see if there's a way to write complicated functions \(f(x)\) in terms of simpler functions \(1,x,x^{2},\dots\text{.}\) To see how, let \(f(x)\) be a function. Our goal is to write it in the form

\begin{equation} f(x) = c_{0}+c_{1}x+c_{2}x^{2}+c_{3}x^{3}+\dots = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}c_{k}x^{k}\tag{8} \end{equation}

where the \(c_{k}\) are constants.
