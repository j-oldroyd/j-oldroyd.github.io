Each tool that we used for solving second order linear ODEs with constant coefficients, in other words those ODEs of the form \(ay''+by'+cy=f(x)\text{,}\) can be extended to solving more general \(n^{\text{th}}\) order linear ODEs with constant coefficients, which take the form

\begin{equation} a_{n}y^{(n)}+a_{n-1}y^{(n-1)}+\dots+a_{1}y'+a_{0}y=f(x)\tag{5} \end{equation}

where \(a_{i},0\leq i\leq n\) are constants and \(a_{n}\neq0\text{.}\) In this section, we'll focus on solutions of \textit{homogeneous} \(n^{\th}\) order linear ODEs with constant coefficients. These are the ODEs where \(f(x) = 0\) in (5).
