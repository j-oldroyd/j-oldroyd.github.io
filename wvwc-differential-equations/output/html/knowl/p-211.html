If we let \(x(t)\) denote the displacement of the mass from the spring’s equilibrium position and let \(F_{S} = -kx\) denote the force of the spring on the mass (see Theorem 2.1.3, and assume that no other force is acting on the mass, then we know from Example 2.1.4 that \(x(t)\) satisfies the second-order ODE given by
\begin{equation*} mx'' + kx = 0. \end{equation*}
If we set \(\omega_{0} = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}\text{,}\) we can rewrite this to get
\begin{equation*} x'' + \omega_{0}^{2}x = 0. \end{equation*}
