Our goal is to find a way of dealing with situations where \(x=0\) is a singular point of an ODE of the form
\begin{equation} x^{2}y''+xp(x)y^\prime+q(x)y=0\text{.}\tag{5.16} \end{equation}
We know, in general, that we won’t be able to find a power series solution \(\sum_{n\geq0}^{}c_{n}x^{n}\text{;}\) intuitively, a power series solution is too “nice” to be a solution of this ODE if \(x=0\) is a singular point.
