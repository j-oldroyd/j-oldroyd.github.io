To fix this, we change our guess for \(y\) to \(y=x^{r}\sum_{n\geq0}^{}c_{n}x^{n}\) or, equivalently,
\begin{equation*} y = \sum_{n\geq0}^{}c_{n}x^{n+r}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Here, \(r\) can be any number (real or complex!), so in general the solution \(y\) produced by this method will not be a power series. We lose a little bit by no longer assuming that \(y\) is a power series, but this expression may be flexible enough to lead to a solution of the ODE at a singular point.
