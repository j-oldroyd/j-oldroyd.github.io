The problem here is that our guess for \(y_{p}\) overlaps with \(y_{c}\text{.}\) To fix this, we’ll multiply our guess for \(y_{p}\) by the smallest power of \(x\) that removes the overlap. In this case, we’ll just multiply by \(x\) to get
\begin{equation*} y_{p} = x[A\cos(3x)+B\sin(3x)] \end{equation*}
Now, we’ll plug our modified guess into the ODE and set it equal to \(3\cos(3x)\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} 3\cos(3x)& = y_{p}'' + 9y\\ & = -6A\sin(3x)+6B\cos(3x) \end{align*}
So we need \(-6A = 0\) and \(6B = 3\text{,}\) or just \(A = 0,B = \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) Hence
\begin{equation*} y_{p} = x\left[\frac{1}{2}\sin(3x)\right], \end{equation*}
and the general solution is then
\begin{equation*} y = y_{c}+y_{p} = c_{1}\cos(3x)+c_{2}\sin(3x) + \frac{x}{2}\sin(3x). \end{equation*}
