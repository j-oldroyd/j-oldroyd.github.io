Let \(f\) and \(g\) be piecewise continuous functions. The convolution of \(f\) and \(g\text{,}\) denoted by \((f\ast g)(t)\text{,}\) \(f(t)\ast g(t)\) or just \(f\ast g\text{,}\) is defined for \(t\geq0\) by

\begin{equation*} (f\ast g)(t) = \int_{0}^{t} f(\tau)g(t-\tau)\,d\tau. \end{equation*}

The variable \(\tau\) is a dummy variable.
