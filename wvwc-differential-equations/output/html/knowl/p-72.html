This ODE is not separable and we can’t just integrate it (since the right hand side depends on the dependent variable). However, the form of the right hand side suggests a substitution: \(u = x+y+3\text{.}\) This would simplify things quite a bit, leaving us with \(y^\prime=u^{2}\text{.}\) The only problem with this is that \(y^\prime\) depends on \(x\text{,}\) not \(u\text{.}\) We must rewrite \(y^\prime\) in terms of the new variable \(u\text{,}\) which isn’t too bad. Since \(u = x+y+3\text{,}\) we get \(y^\prime = u^\prime-1\text{.}\) Therefore the ODE becomes
\begin{equation*} u^\prime-1 = u^{2}\text{ or just }\dv{u}{x} = 1+u^{2}. \end{equation*}
