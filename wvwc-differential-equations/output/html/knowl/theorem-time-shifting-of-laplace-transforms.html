Theorem 6.3.1. Time Shifting of Laplace Transforms.

Let \(f(t)\) denote a piecewise continuous function with Laplace transform \(F(s)\text{.}\) Let \(a\geq0\text{.}\) Then \(\Laplace{u(t-a)f(t-a)} = e^{-as}F(s)\text{,}\) or equivalently \(\iLaplace{e^{-as}F(s)} = u(t-a)f(t-a)\text{.}\)

Proof.

To compute this we need to rely on the definition of the Laplace transform:
\begin{align*} \Laplace{u(t-a)f(t-a)} &= \int_{0}^{\infty}u(t-a)f(t-a)e^{-st}\,dt\\ &= \int_{a}^{\infty}f(t-a)e^{-st}\,dt\\ &= \int_{0}^{\infty}f(\tau)e^{-s(\tau+a)}\,d\tau\\ &= e^{-sa}\int_{0}^{\infty}f(\tau)e^{-s\tau}\,d\tau\\ &= e^{-as}F(s)\text{.} \end{align*}
