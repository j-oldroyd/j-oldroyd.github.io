An important differential equation in applications is the Legendre equation given by

\begin{equation} (1 - x^{2})y'' - 2xy^\prime + k(k + 1)y = 0\text{.}\tag{10} \end{equation}

Our first example of this equation (with \(n = 1\)) was examined in ExampleÂ 5.9. By this example, we see that (10) has a series solution centered at \(x = 0\) with radius of convergence at least \(1\text{.}\) Therefore the power series method is appropriate.
