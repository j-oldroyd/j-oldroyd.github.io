The most important property of the Dirac delta is that
\begin{equation*} \int_{\alpha}^{\beta}\delta(t-a)\dd{t} = \begin{cases} 1 \amp\text{if }\alpha\leq a\leq\beta \\ 0 \amp\text{otherwise.} \end{cases} \end{equation*}
This property can be generalized to products involving the Dirac delta and continuous functions. Very roughly, if \(\delta(t - a)\) represents an arrow with an “area” of \(1\) concentrated at \(t = a\text{,}\) then \(\delta(t - a)g(t)\) represents an arrow with area \(g(a)\) concentrated at \(t = a\text{.}\) This sampling property is stated more precisely as Theorem 6.4.4.
