You may be troubled by the fact that we only explicitly used the first initial condition in this example. However, the second condition \(x'(0) = 0\) was actually used implicitly: it allowed us to assume that the amplitude was \(5\) as opposed to another number. If \(x'(0)\neq0\text{,}\) finding \(C\) would have been a little trickier.
