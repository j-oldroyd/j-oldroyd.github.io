Let \(f(x) = \sin(mx)\) and \(g(x) = \cos(nx)\) where \(m,n > 0\text{.}\) Suppose that \(\frac{m}{n} = \frac{a}{b}\in\QQ\) where \(\frac{a}{b}\) represents the reduced fraction of \(\frac{m}{n}\text{.}\) Then \(f(x) + g(x)\) has period given by
\begin{equation*} T = \frac{2\pi}{(\frac{m}{a})} = \frac{2\pi}{(\frac{n}{b})}\text{.} \end{equation*}
