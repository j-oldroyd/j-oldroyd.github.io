The initial conditions are \(x(0) = 3\) and \(x'(0) = 0\text{,}\) which give the equations

\begin{align*} 3 \amp = c_{1} + c_{2} \\ 0 \amp = -2ic_{1} + 2ic_{2} \end{align*}

The second equation implies that \(c_{1} = c_{2}\text{,}\) and applying this to the first equation now gives \(c_{1} = c_{2} = \frac{3}{2}\text{.}\) Hence the displacement of the mass is given by

\begin{equation*} x = \frac{3}{2}e^{-2it} + \frac{3}{2}e^{2it}. \end{equation*}
