Solutions of linear, homogeneous ODEs with constant coefficients depend entirely on the roots of the corresponding characteristic equation. If we write the independent variable as \(x\text{,}\) and if \(r_{k}\) denotes a single root of the characteristic equation, then the general solution of the ODE will contain \(e^{r_{k}x},xe^{r_{k}x},x^{2}e^{r_{k}x},\dots\text{,}\) with the number of exponentials contributed by \(r_{k}\) being equal to its multiplicity. That is, the number of times \(r_{k}\) appears as a solution of the characteristic equation.
