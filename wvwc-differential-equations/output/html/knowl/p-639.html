A trigonometric polynomial is a finite sum of the form
\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^{n}(a_{k}\cos kx+b_{k}\sin kx) = a_{0} + \sum_{k=1}^{n}(a_{k}\cos kx+b_{k}\sin kx) \end{equation*}
for some natural number \(n\text{.}\) A trigonometric series is a series of the form
\begin{equation*} \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}(a_{k}\cos kx+b_{k}\sin kx) = a_{0} + \sum_{k=1}^{\infty}(a_{k}\cos kx+b_{k}\sin kx)\text{.} \end{equation*}
For both sums the values \(a_{k},b_{k}\) are constants called the coefficients.
