Just as in the previous sections, the phase plane is still the \(y_{1}y_{2}-plane\text{,}\) trajectories are still the solutions \(\vec{y}\) of (4.2) (represented as curves in the phase plane), and the phase portrait of (4.2) is the set of all trajectories in the phase plane. We call a point \(P\) in the phase plane a critical point of (4.2) if it is a solution of the same system. Equivalently, the critical points of (4.2) are precisely those points \((y_{1},y_{2})\) for which
\begin{equation*} f_{1}(y_{1},y_{2}) = 0\quad\text{and}\quad f_{2}(y_{1},y_{2}) = 0. \end{equation*}
