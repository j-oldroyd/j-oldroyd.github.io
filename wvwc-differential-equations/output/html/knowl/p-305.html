So our initial guess for \(y_{p}\) is given by \(y_{p} = C_{1}x^{2}+C_{2}x+C_{3}e^{-3x}\cos x+C_{4}e^{-3x}\sin x\text{.}\) Plugging this into the ODE into a CAS such as Maple or Sage gives
\begin{align*} x+e^{-3x}\sin x \amp= y^{(3)}_{p}-2y''_{p}+3y^\prime_{p}\\ \amp= 6C_{1}x+(3C_{2}-4C_{1}) \\ \amp\quad + \parens{-43C_{3}+41C_{4}}e^{-3x}\cos x+\parens{-41C_{3}-43C_{4}}e^{-3x}\sin x \end{align*}
This gives us the system of equations
\begin{align*} 6C_{1} \amp= 1\\ 3C_{2}-4C_{1} \amp= 0\\ -43C_{3}+41C_{4} \amp= 0\\ -41C_{3}-43C_{4} \amp= 1 \end{align*}
which we can solve using Sage to get
\begin{equation*} C_{1} = \frac{1}{6},C_{2} = \frac{2}{9},C_{3} = -\frac{41}{3530},C_{4}=-\frac{43}{3530}. \end{equation*}
