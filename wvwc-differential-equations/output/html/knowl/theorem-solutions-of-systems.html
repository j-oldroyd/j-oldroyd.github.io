Theorem 4.2.1. Solutions of Systems.

Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) constant matrix, and suppose that \(A\) has \(n\) linearly independent eigenvectors \(\vec{x}_{1},\dots,\vec{x}_{n}\) with corresponding eigenvalues \(\lambda_{1},\dots,\lambda_{1}\text{.}\) Then the general solution of \(\vec{x}^\prime=A\vec{x}\) is given by

\begin{equation*} \vec{x} = \sum_{i=1}^{n}c_{i}e^{\lambda_{i}t}\vec{x}_{i}. \end{equation*}
