This allows us (theoretically) to solve for \(k\) and the coefficients \(C_n\text{.}\) In fact, we get
\begin{align*} k \amp= 0\\ C_{2n} \amp= \frac{(-1)^n}{(2n)!}C_0\\ C_{2n+1} \amp= \frac{(-1)^n}{(2n+1)!}C_1 \end{align*}
and so
\begin{equation*} y_2 = x^{-1/2}\left[C_0\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^n}{(2n)!}x^{2n} + C_1 \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^n}{(2n+1)!}x^{2n+1}\right]\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since the second series corresponds to a multiple of \(y_1 = \frac{\sin(x)}{\sqrt{x}}\text{,}\) we can safely set \(C_1 = 0\) and get \(y_2 = C_0\frac{\cos(x)}{\sqrt{x}}\text{.}\) Therefore the general solution of the ODE is
\begin{equation*} y = A_1\frac{\sin(x)}{\sqrt{x}} + A_{2}\frac{\cos(x)}{\sqrt{x}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
