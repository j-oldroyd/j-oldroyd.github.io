The solution of this looks like \(\vec{y}(t) = \begin{bmatrix}y_{1}(t) \\ y_{2}(t)\end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) As \(t\) varies, \(\vec{y}(t)\) will trace out a curve in the \(y_{1}y_{2}\)-plane, which we call the trajectory. The \(y_{1}y_{2}\)-plane is called the phase plane, and the collection of all trajectories of the system (6) is called the phase portrait. The phase portrait of a system provides us with a way to study the behavior of solutions of (6) without actually solving the system.
