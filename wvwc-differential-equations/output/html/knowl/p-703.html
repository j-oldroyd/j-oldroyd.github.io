Given some quantity \(u(x)\) that depends solely on the variable \(x\text{,}\) \(\frac{du}{dx} = u^{\prime}(x)\) represents the rate of change of \(u\) with respect to \(x\text{.}\) More generally, given some quantity \(u(x,t)\) that depends on \(x,t\text{,}\) we can attempt to find the rate of change of \(u\) with respect to each of the variables \(x,t\text{.}\) This idea leads to partial derivatives.
