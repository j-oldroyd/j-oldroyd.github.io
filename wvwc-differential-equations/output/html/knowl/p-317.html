Let \(A\) be the \(2\times 2\) matrix
\begin{equation*} A = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11}& a_{12}\\a_{21}& a_{22} \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
and let \(\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_{1}\\v_{2} \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Then their product \(A\vec{v}\) is the vector defined to be
\begin{equation*} A\vec{v} = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11}v_{1}+a_{12}v_{2}\\a_{21}v_{1}+a_{22}v_{2} \end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
The \(2\times 2\) identity matrix is the matrix \(I = \begin{bmatrix}1&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) A scalar is just a constant. To multiply a scalar \(c\) with a matrix \(A\text{,}\) just multiply every element of \(A\) with \(c\text{.}\)
