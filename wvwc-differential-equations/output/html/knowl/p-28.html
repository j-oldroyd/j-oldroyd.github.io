It may not be obvious that Newton's Law of Cooling can be restated as a differential equation, but the phrase "rate of change" that appears in the statement of the law is a good clue that this can be done. To do this, first we need to give the relevant quantities (the temperature of the object and the temperature of the surrounding medium) names. Let \(T(t)\) denote the temperature of the object at time \(t\) and let \(A(t)\) denote the temperature of the surrounding medium at time \(t\text{.}\) Then Newton's Law of Cooling says that

\begin{equation*} \dv{T}{t} = k(T-A) \end{equation*}

where \(k\) is some constant.
