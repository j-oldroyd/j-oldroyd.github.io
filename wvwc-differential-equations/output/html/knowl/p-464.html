Power series were introduced in calculus to allow us to write complicated functions \(f(x)\) in terms of simpler functions \(1,x,x^{2},\dots\text{.}\) In particular, our goal is to write \(f(x)\) in the form
\begin{equation} f(x) = c_{0}+c_{1}x+c_{2}x^{2}+c_{3}x^{3}+\dots = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}c_{k}x^{k}\tag{5.2} \end{equation}
where the coefficients \(c_{k}\) are all constants.
