There are a few of interesting things we can note about the Example 2.1.10. First, each eigenvalue had at least one corresponding eigenvector. Second, \(\lambda=3\) was basically a “repeated” eigenvalue, since it showed up as a double root in the characteristic equation \(\det(A-\lambda I)=0\text{.}\) Third, the eigenvalue \(\lambda=3\) has essentially two distinct eigenvectors corresponding to it, in the sense that every other possible eigenvector for \(\lambda=3\) is a linear combination of \(\vb{v}_{2}\) and \(\vb{v}_{3}\text{.}\) This leads us to some terminology.
