Definition 2.1.4. Eigenspaces.

Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix and suppose that \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\text{.}\) The eigenspace of \(A\) corresponding to \(\lambda\) is the subspace of \(\RR^{n}\) containing all of the eigenvectors corresponding to \(\lambda\) in addition to the zero vector. In other words, the eigenspace of \(A\) corresponding to \(\lambda\) is the set \(\null(A-\lambda I)\text{.}\)
