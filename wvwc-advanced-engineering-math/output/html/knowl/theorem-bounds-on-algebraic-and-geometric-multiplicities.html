Theorem 2.1.13. Bounds on Algebraic and Geometric Multiplicities.

If \(A\) is an \(n\times n\) matrix, then \(A\) has exactly \(n\) (not necessarily distinct!) eigenvalues, counting multiplicities. Furthermore, if \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(A\text{,}\) then the geometric multiplicity is always less than or equal to the algebraic multiplicity.
