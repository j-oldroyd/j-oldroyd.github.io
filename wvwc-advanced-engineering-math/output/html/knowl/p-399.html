The definition given in DefinitionÂ 4.1.2, while important, will not typically be used for computing line integrals. Instead, we will take another approach using arc length. The differential \(\dd{s}\) in the line integral formula corresponds to the arc length function along \(C\text{.}\) Therefore we may write
\begin{equation*} \dd{s} = \norm{\rrp(t)}\dd{t} \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation} \int_C f(x, y)\dd{s} = \int_a^b f(\rr(t))\norm{\rrp(t)}\dd{t}\tag{1} \end{equation}
for some suitable smooth parameterization \(\rr(t), a\leq t\leq b\) of \(C\text{.}\)
