The formula in DefinitionÂ 4.1.5 can be simplified in much the same way as the original definition of a scalar line integral was in (1). First, suppose that \(C\) is parameterized by the smooth vector function \(\rr(t):[a,b]\to\RR^n\text{.}\) Then
\begin{align*} \FF\cdot\TT\dd{s} \amp = \FF(\rr(t))\cdot\frac{\rrp(t)}{\norm{\rrp(t)}}\norm{\rrp(t)}\dd{t} \\ \amp = \FF(\rr(t))\cdot\rrp(t)\dd{t} \\ \amp = \FF\cdot\dd{\rr} \text{.} \end{align*}
Therefore
\begin{equation} \int_C\FF\cdot\TT\dd{s} = \int_a^b\FF\cdot\dd{\rr}\text{.}\tag{2} \end{equation}
