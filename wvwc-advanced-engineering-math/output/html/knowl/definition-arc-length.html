Definition 3.3.1. Arc Length.

Let \(\rr(t)\) be a smooth space curve defined over the interval \(I\) and let \(a,b\in I\text{.}\) The arc length of \(\rr(t)\) from \(t = a\) to \(t = b\) is the integral

\begin{equation*} s = \int_a^b \norm{\rrp(t)}\dd{t}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The arc length formula is a generalization of the simple \(d = st\) formula from introductory physics. Here, the speed \(s\) is replaced with \(\norm{\rrp(t)}\) and the time \(t\) is replaced with \(\dd{t}\text{.}\) The integral can be viewed as a “continuous sum” of the possible products of speed and time over the interval \([a,b]\text{,}\) and therefore gives the length or total distance traveled along the curve.
