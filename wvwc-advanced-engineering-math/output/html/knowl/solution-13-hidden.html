We can find \(L\) and \(U\) by reducing \(A\) to an echelon form. One possibility is

\begin{equation*} L = \mqty[1 & 0 & 0 \\ -2 & 1 & 0 \\ -1 & -4 & 1]\text{ and }U = \mqty[-1 & -7 & -3 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -1]. \end{equation*}

Now we can solve \(A\vb{x} = \vb{b}\) in two steps:

  1. Solve \(L\vb{y} = \vb{b}\) for \(\vb{y}\text{.}\)

  2. Solve \(U\vb{x} = \vb{y}\) for \(\vb{x}\text{.}\)

Solving the first equation by reducing \(\smqty[L & \vb{b}]\) produces

\begin{equation*} \vb{y} = \mqty[1 \\ -3 \\ 0]\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then, solving the second equation by reducing \(\smqty[U & \vb{y}]\) produces

\begin{equation*} \vb{x} = \mqty[20\\-3\\0]. \end{equation*}
