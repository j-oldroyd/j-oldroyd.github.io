Theorem 3.4.5. Gradients and Maximum Rates of Change.

Let \(f\) be a continuously differentiable scalar field and suppose that \(\grad f\neq\vb{0}\) (so that \(\grad f\) is smooth). Then \(\grad f\) is parallel to the direction of greatest increase of \(f\text{,}\) and the corresponding rate of increase is \(\norm{\grad{f}}\text{.}\)
