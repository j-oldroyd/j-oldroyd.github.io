Example 3.6.3. Computing a Laplacian.

The Laplacian of a scalar field \(f(x, y, z)\) is the scalar field \(\del^2 f\) where \(\del^2 = \div\del\text{.}\) In other words,

\begin{equation*} \del^2 f = \divt(\del f)\text{.} \end{equation*}
