Theorem 3.4.3. Gradients and Directional Derivatives.

Let \(f(x,y,z)\) be a continuously differentiable scalar field and let \(\vv\in\RR^3\) be a nonzero vector. The directional derivative of \(f\) in the direction of \(\vv\) is
\begin{equation*} D_{\vv}f = \grad f \cdot \frac{\vv}{\norm{\vv}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
