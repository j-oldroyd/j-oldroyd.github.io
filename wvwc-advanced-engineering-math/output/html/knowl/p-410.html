First, note that \(C\) is a smooth curve on which \(\FF\) is continuous and the line integral of \(\FF\) along \(C\) is therefore defined. By (2), it follows that
\begin{equation*} \int_C\FF\cdot\TT\dd{s} = \int_1^{10}\FF\cdot\dd{\rr} \end{equation*}
where
\begin{align*} \FF \amp = \FF(\rr(t)) = \frac{1}{17t^2}\mqty[t \amp 4t] \\ \dd{\rr} \amp = \rrp(t)\dd{t} = \mqty[1 \amp 4]\dd{t} \text{.} \end{align*}
Therefore
\begin{align*} \int_1^{10}\FF\cdot\dd{\rr} \amp = \int_1^{10}\frac{1}{17t^2}(t + 16t)\dd{t} \\ \amp = \int_1^{10}\frac{1}{t}\dd{t} \\ \amp = \ln(10) \text{.} \end{align*}
