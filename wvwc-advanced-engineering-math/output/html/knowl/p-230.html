In the mathematics of signal processing, signals are often represented as particular vectors in \(\RR^n\text{.}\) The problem of signal transmission then reduces to sending a list of numbers \(c_1,\ldots, c_m\) such that the receiver can use these to reconstruct a signal \(\xx\text{.}\) Such a scheme can be implemented by choosing an appropriate \(n\times m\) matrix \(F = \smqty[\vb{f}_1 \amp \ldots \amp \vb{f}_m]\) and then computing and transmitting the coordinates of

\begin{equation*} F^T\xx = \mqty[\langle \xx,\vb{f}_i\rangle]_{1\leq i\leq m}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The important properties of the columns of \(F\) can then be encoded in the Gram matrix \(G = F^TF = \mqty[\dotprod{\vb{f}_i,\vb{f}_j}]\text{.}\) Find a \(3\times 4\) matrix \(F\) for which that Gram matrix is

\begin{equation*} G = \mqty[1 \amp -\frac{1}{3} \amp -\frac{1}{3} \amp -\frac{1}{3} \\ -\frac{1}{3} \amp 1 \amp -\frac{1}{3} \amp -\frac{1}{3} \\ -\frac{1}{3} \amp -\frac{1}{3} \amp 1 \amp -\frac{1}{3} \\ -\frac{1}{3} \amp -\frac{1}{3} \amp -\frac{1}{3} \amp 1]\text{.} \end{equation*}
