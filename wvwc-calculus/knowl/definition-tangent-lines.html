Definition 2.1.2. Tangent Lines.

The tangent line to a curve \(y=f(x)\) through a point \((a,f(a))\) is the line passing through \((a,f(a))\) with slope given by

\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to a}\frac{f(x)-f(a)}{x-a}, \end{equation*}

assuming this limit exists. The slope of the tangent line represents the slope of the graph of \(f(x)\) at \(a\) and gives the instantaneous rate of change of \(f(x)\) at \(x=a\text{.}\)
