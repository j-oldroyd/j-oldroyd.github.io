Definition 11.7.4. Derivatives of Vector Functions.

Let \(\mathbf{r}(t)\) denote a vector function. The derivative of \(\mathbf{r}\) is the new vector function \(\mathbf{r}'\) given by

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{r}'(t) = \frac{d\mathbf{r}}{dt} = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{\mathbf{r}(t+h)-\mathbf{r}(t)}{h}, \end{equation*}

assuming that the limit exists. If this limit exists, we say that \(\mathbf{r}\) is differentiable.
