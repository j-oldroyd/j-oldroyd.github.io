Example 11.6.5. Region between surfaces.

Suppose we want to sketch the region between the surface \(z=\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}}\) and \(x^{2}+y^{2}=1\) for \(1\leq z\leq 2\text{.}\) First, we can graph \(z=\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}}\text{.}\) If we look at the horizontal traces of this surface, we get circles of varying radii. As \(z\) increases, the radii of these circles increase as well. This surface is just a cone! So we're describing the region of this cone bounded between \(z=1\) and \(z=2\text{,}\) and contained inside the cylinder \(x^{2}+y^{2}=1\text{.}\)
