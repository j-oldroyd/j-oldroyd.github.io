The quantity \(\pdv{Q}{x} - \pdv{P}{y}\) in TheoremÂ 14.3.7 is called the (two-dimensional) curl of \(\vb{F}\) and is denoted by \(\curlt \vb{F}\text{.}\) This represents the tendency of \(\vb{F}\) to rotate counterclockwise around a given point. This can be extended to three dimensions using the following formula:

\begin{equation*} \curlt\vb{F} = \curl\vb{F} \end{equation*}

where \(\nabla = \dotprod{\pdv{}{x}, \pdv{}{y}, \pdv{}{z}}\text{.}\) The direction of \(\curl\vb{F}\) provides the axis of rotation at a point, and its magnitude is the tendency of \(\vb{F}\) to rotate counterclockwise around this axis of rotation (viewed head on).
