First, we'll try to figure out what our equations should look like. We know that \(x\) starts at \(1\) and \(y\) starts at \(-3\) on this segment, so a reasonable guess is

\begin{align*} x \amp = 1 + at \\ y \amp = -3 + bt \end{align*}

for some unknown constants \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) To find \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) we'll specify the interval of values we want \(t\) to range over. To make things easier, let's fix \(t\) between \(0\) and \(1\text{.}\) Then at \(t = 1\) we need to have

\begin{align*} x \amp = 1 + a = -2 \\ y \amp = -3 + b = 4 \end{align*}

which gives \(a = -3\) and \(b = 7\text{.}\) Therefore the line segment is parameterized by

\begin{align*} x \amp = 1 - 3t \\ y \amp = -3 + 7t \\ 0 \amp \leq t\leq 1 \text{.} \end{align*}
