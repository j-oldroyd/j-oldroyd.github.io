Theorem 12.7.4 is useful for finding local maxima and minima of a function \(f(x,y)\text{,}\) but it's often the case where we have an additional restriction on the variables \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) which we interpret as an equality \(g(x,y) = k\) for some constant \(k\text{.}\) Geometrically, this reduces down to finding the maximum and minimum values of \(f(x,y)\) on the curve \(g(x,y) = k\text{.}\) In these cases, Theorem 12.7.4 is not as useful because there's no guarantee that the critical points of \(f\) will lie on the curve \(g(x,y) = k\text{.}\) For this scenario, we must use the method of Lagrange multipliers.
