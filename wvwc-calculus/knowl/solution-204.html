Solution 10.1.4.1.

This problem has a lot to unpack, but we know the basic form of our equations will be

\begin{equation*} x = 3 + 3\cos t\qq{and} y = -2 + 3\sin t\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since we want to start at \((6,-2)\text{,}\) we can let \(t = 0\) be our starting value. And since we want to move around the circle \(3\) times, we'll let \(t\) vary from \(0\) to \(6\pi\text{.}\) Finally, to get clockwise motion we need to replace \(t\) with \(-t\text{,}\) getting

\begin{align*} x \amp = 3 + 3\cos t \\ y \amp = -2 - 3\sin t \end{align*}

for \(0\leq t\leq 6\pi\text{.}\)
