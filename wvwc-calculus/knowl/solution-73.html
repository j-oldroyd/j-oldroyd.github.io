Solution 4.2.2.1

The speed of the car can be represented by a velocity function \(v(t)\text{.}\) If we assume that the car entered the highway at time \(t_{\text{in}}\) and left at some future time \(t_{\text{out}}\text{,}\) then we know that

\begin{equation*} v(t_{\text{in}}) = v(t_{\text{out}}) = 45. \end{equation*}

By TheoremÂ 4.2.1, there must be some time \(t_{1}\) between \(t_{\text{in}}\) and \(t_{\text{out}}\) for which \(v'(t_{1}) = 0\text{.}\) Since \(v'\) is exactly the acceleration, we know that the car had to stop accelerating somewhere on the highway.
