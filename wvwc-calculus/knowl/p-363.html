We start with a theorem that is very reasonable, at least geometrically. Let \(f(x)\) be a differentiable function on some interval \([a,b]\text{,}\) and suppose \(f(a) = f(b)\text{.}\) Then this means the graph of \(f\) must "turn around" at some point in \([a,b]\text{,}\) i.e., there is a local maximum or minimum contained within the interval.
