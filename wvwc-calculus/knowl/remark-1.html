Remark 9.3.7

It's usually easier to use Definition 9.3.2 to compute dot products. However, Theorem 9.3.5 gives us extremely useful geometric information about the dot product. For example, we get the following result very quickly: two vectors \(\vv{a}\) and \(\vv{b}\) are perpendicular if and only if \(\vv{a}\cdot\vv{b} = 0\text{.}\)
