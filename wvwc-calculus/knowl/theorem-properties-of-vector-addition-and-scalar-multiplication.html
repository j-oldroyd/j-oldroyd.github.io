Theorem 11.2.7. Properties of Vector Addition and Scalar Multiplication.

Let \(\vv{x},\vv{y},\vv{z}\) be vectors and let \(\alpha,\beta\) be scalars. Then the following are true:

  1. \(\vv{x}+\vv{y} = \vv{y}+\vv{x}\text{.}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \vv{x}+(\vv{y}+\vv{z}) = (\vv{x}+\vv{y})+\vv{z}.\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \vv{x}+\vv{0} = \vv{x}.\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \vv{x}+(-\vv{x}) = \vv{0}.\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \alpha(\vv{x}+\vv{y}) = \alpha\vv{x}+\alpha\vv{y}.\)

  6. \(\displaystyle (\alpha+\beta)\vv{x} = \alpha\vv{x}+\beta\vv{y}\)

  7. \(\displaystyle (\alpha\beta)\vv{x} = \alpha(\beta)\vv{x}.\)

  8. \(\displaystyle 1\vv{x} = \vv{x}.\)
