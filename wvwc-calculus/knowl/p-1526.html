In SectionÂ 9.3, we saw that introducing a new coordinate system made certain double integrals much easier to work with. The same idea works with triple integrals. The first such system we'll look at is cylindrical coordinates, which are useful for computing triple integrals over cylindrical regions. To convert Cartesian coordinates \((x,y,z)\) into cylindrical coordinates \((r,\theta,z)\text{,}\) simply replace \(x,y\) with polar coordinates, and use \(dV = r\,dr\,d\theta\,dz\text{.}\) We leave \(z\) alone.
