If \(C:\vb{r}(t)\text{,}\) then \(\vb{r}'(t)\) gives the tangent vector to \(C\) at the point corresponding to \(\vb{r}(t)\text{.}\) In the same way, if a surface \(S\) is given by \(\vb{r}(u,v)\text{,}\) then

\begin{align*} \pdv{\vb{r}}{u} \amp = \vb{r}_{u} \\ \pdv{\vb{r}}{v} \amp = \vb{r}_{v} \end{align*}

give tangent vectors to \(S\) at the point corresponding to \(\vb{r}(u,v)\text{.}\) \(\vb{r}_{u}\) gives the tangent in the direction of increasing \(u\text{,}\) while \(\vb{r}_{v}\) gives the tangent in the direction of increasing \(v\text{.}\) We say that \(S\) is smooth if \(\vb{r}_{u}\) and \(\vb{r}_{v}\) exist, are continuous and are never \(\vb{0}\text{.}\) In this case, we can construct the tangent plane to \(S\text{.}\)
