Solution 6.6.4.1.

Since \(n(t)\) is the rate of change of the mass of the substance, the mass itself must be an antiderivative of \(n(t)\text{.}\) Therefore the mass decreases by

\begin{equation*} \int_{0}^{1}n(t)\,dt = \frac{1 - e^{-1}}{2} \end{equation*}

over the interval \([0,1]\text{.}\)
