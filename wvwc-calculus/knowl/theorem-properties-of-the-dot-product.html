Theorem 11.3.4. Properties of the Dot Product.

Let \(\vv{x},\vv{y}\) and \(\vv{z}\) be vectors, and let \(\alpha\) be a scalar. Then the following properties hold:

  1. \(\displaystyle \vv{x}\cdot\vv{x} = \norm{\vv{x}}^{2}.\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \vv{x}\cdot\vv{y} = \vv{y}\cdot\vv{x}.\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \vv{x}\cdot(\vv{y}+\vv{z}) = \vv{x}\cdot\vv{y}+\vv{x}\cdot\vv{z}.\)

  4. \(\displaystyle (\alpha\vv{x})\cdot\vv{y} = \alpha(\vv{x}\cdot\vv{y}) = \vv{x}\cdot(\alpha\vv{y}).\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \vv{0}\cdot\vv{x} = 0.\)
