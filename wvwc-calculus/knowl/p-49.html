Let \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) be continuous at a point \(a\text{.}\) Then the following statements are true:

  1. \(f(x)+g(x)\) is continuous at \(a\text{.}\)
  2. \(f(x)g(x)\) is continuous at \(a\text{.}\)
  3. \(\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\) is continuous at \(a\) if \(g(a)\neq0\text{.}\)
  4. \(f(g(x))\) is continuous at \(a\) if \(g(a)\) is in the domain of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
