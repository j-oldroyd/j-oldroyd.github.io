By the product rule, we have \(\dv{}{x}[f(x)g(x)] = f(x)g'(x) + f'(x)g(x)\text{.}\) Rearranging this, we have

\begin{equation*} f(x)g'(x) = [f(x)g(x)]' - f'(x)g(x). \end{equation*}

Finally, we can integrate both sides to get

\begin{equation*} \int f(x)g'(x)\,dx = f(x)g(x) - \int g(x)f'(x)\,dx. \end{equation*}
