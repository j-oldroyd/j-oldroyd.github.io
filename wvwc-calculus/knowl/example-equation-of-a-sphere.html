Example 11.1.7. Equation of a sphere.

The equation \(2x^{2}+2y^{2}+2z^{2} = 8x - 24z + 4\) represents a sphere in \(\RR^{3}\text{.}\) To see how, we can rearrange the equation and complete the square to get

\begin{align*} 2x^{2}+2y^{2}+2z^{2} - 8x + 24z = 4 & \Rightarrow x^{2}+y^{2}+z^{2} - 4x + 12z = 2 \\ & \Rightarrow (x-2)^{2} + y^{2} + (z-6)^{2} = 2 + 4 + 36\\ & \Rightarrow (x-2)^{2} + y^{2} + (z-6)^{2} = 42. \end{align*}

So this equation describes a sphere of radius \(\sqrt{42}\) centered at \((2,0,6)\text{.}\)
