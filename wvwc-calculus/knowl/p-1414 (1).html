From Calculus I, we know how to find the area under the graph \(y = f(x)\) from \(x = a\) to \(x = b\text{:}\) it's just

\begin{equation*} \int_{a}^{b}f(x)\,dx. \end{equation*}

What we want to do now is to take the notion of integration and extend it to higher dimensions. As a starting motivation, we'd like to develop the concept of an integral of a function \(f(x,y)\) of two independent variables in order to find the volume under the surface \(z = f(x,y)\text{.}\)
