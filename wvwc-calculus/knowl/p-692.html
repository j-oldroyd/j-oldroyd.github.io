Suppose that the first mass is \(d_{1}\) units away from the fulcrum, and that the second mass is \(d_{2}\) units away. Archimedes' Law of the Lever states that the rod will balance if \(m_{1}d_{1} = m_{2}d_{2}\text{.}\) Since \(d_{1} = c - x_{1}\) and \(d_{2} = x_{2} - c\) (since \(d_{1},d_{2}\) are positive!), we can rewrite this as

\begin{align*} m_{1}d_{1} \amp= m_{2}d_{2}\\ \amp\Rightarrow\\ m_{1}(c-x_{1}) \amp= m_{2}(x_{2}-c)\\ \amp\Rightarrow\\ m_{1}c-m_{1}x_{1} \amp= m_{2}x_{2} - m_{2}c\\ \amp\Rightarrow\\ m_{1}c+m_{2}c \amp= m_{1}x_{1}+m_{2}x_{2} \end{align*}

which simplifies down to

\begin{equation*} c = \frac{m_{1}x_{1}+m_{2}x_{2}}{m_{1}+m_{2}}. \end{equation*}

Hence \(c\) is the location of the center of mass of the rod if this equation is true!
