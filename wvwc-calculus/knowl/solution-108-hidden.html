This integral looks awful, but the integrand contains \(\ln x\) and its derivative \(\frac{1}{x}\text{,}\) which suggests that the substitution \(u = \ln x\) might simplify things. So let \(u = \ln x\text{.}\) Then \(du = \frac{1}{x}\,dx\text{,}\) or \(dx = x\,du\text{,}\) and the integral becomes

\begin{align*} \int_{e^{2}}^{e^{4}}\frac{dx}{x\sqrt{\ln x}} & \int_{x=e^{2}}^{x=e^{4}}\frac{x\,du}{x\sqrt{u}} \\ & = \int_{x=e^{2}}^{x=e^{4}}\frac{du}{\sqrt{u}} \\ & = \Big[u\Big]_{x=e^{2}}^{x=e^{4}} \\ & = \Big[\ln x\Big]_{e^{2}}^{e^{4}} \\ & = 2. \end{align*}
