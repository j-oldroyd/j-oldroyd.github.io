At this point, we can use \(u\)-substitution to find each integral, using \(u = -x^{2}\) and \(du = -2x\,dx\text{.}\) So we get

\begin{align*} \lim_{a\to\-\infty}\int_{a}^{0}xe^{-x^{2}}\,dx + \lim_{b\to\infty}\int_{0}^{b}xe^{-x^{2}} & = \lim_{a\to\-\infty}\int_{-a^{2}}^{0}\frac{-e^{u}}{2}\,du + \lim_{b\to\infty}\int_{0}^{-b^{2}}\frac{-e^{u}}{2}\,du \\ & = \lim_{a\to-\infty}\left[-\frac{1}{2} + \frac{e^{-a^{2}}}{2}\right] + \lim_{b\to\infty}\left[-\frac{e^{-b^{2}}}{2} + \frac{1}{2}\right]\\ & = \lim_{a\to\infty}\frac{e^{-a^{2}}}{2} - \lim_{b\to\infty}\frac{e^{-b^{2}}}{2} \\ & = 0. \end{align*}
