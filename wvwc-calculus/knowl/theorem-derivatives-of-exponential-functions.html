Theorem 4.3.1. Derivatives of Exponential Functions.

Let $a > 0$ and set $f(x) = a^{x}\text{.}$ Then $f'(x) = a^{x}\ln a\text{.}$

Proof.

To prove this, we need to use what's available to us: the derivative of $e^{x}\text{.}$ From ExampleÂ 4.2.11 we say that $e^{x\ln 3} = 3^{x}\text{.}$ More generally, we can also say that $e^{x\ln a} = a^{x}\text{.}$ Hence

\begin{align*} \dv{}{x}a^{x} & = \dv{}{x}e^{x\ln a} \\ & = (\ln a)e^{x\ln a} \\ & = (\ln a)a^{x}. \end{align*}
