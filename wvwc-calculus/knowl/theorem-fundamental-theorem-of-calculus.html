Theorem 5.4.2 Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Let \(f(x)\) be a continuous function defined on \([a,b]\text{.}\)

  1. Define \(g(x) = \int_{a}^{x}f(t)\,dt\) for \(x\) in \([a,x]\text{.}\) Then \(g'(x) = f(x)\text{,}\) i.e., \(g\) is an antiderivative of \(f\text{.}\)
  2. Let \(F\) be a particular antiderivative of \(f\text{.}\) Then \(\int_{a}^{b}f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a).\)
