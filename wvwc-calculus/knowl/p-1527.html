The mass is given by

\begin{align*} \iint_{D} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x^{2}+y^{2}}}\,dA & = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{1}^{2}\frac{r}{r}\,dr\,d\theta \\ & = 2\pi \end{align*}

The moments are

\begin{align*} M_{x} & = \iint_{D}\frac{y}{\sqrt{x^{2} + y^{2}}}\,dA \\ & = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{1}^{2} \frac{r\sin\theta}{r}r\,dr\,d\theta \\ & = 0 \end{align*}

and likewise \(M_{y} = 0\text{.}\) So the center of mass is \((0,0)\text{.}\)
