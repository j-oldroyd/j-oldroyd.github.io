The moments of the mass contained in \(D\) and with density \(\rho(x,y)\) are defined as follows:

\begin{align*} M_{x} & = \iint_{D}y\rho(x,y)\,dA \\ M_{y} & = \iint_{D}x\rho(x,y)\,dA \end{align*}

If we let \(m\) denote the total mass, then we also define the center of mass (or centroid) to be the point \((\bar{x},\bar{y})\text{,}\) where

\begin{align*} \bar{x} & = \frac{M_{y}}{m} \\ \bar{y} & = \frac{M_{x}}{m} \end{align*}
