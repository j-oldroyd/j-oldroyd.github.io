We need to check continuity at \(s=-1\) and \(s=1\text{.}\) At \(s=-1\text{,}\) we need to make sure that \(\lim_{s\to -1}g(s)\) exists and is equal to \(g(-1)\text{.}\) Since

\begin{equation*} \lim_{s\to-1^{-1}}g(s) = \lim_{s\to-1^{-1}}s^{2} = 1 \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} \lim_{s\to-1^{+}}g(s) = \lim_{s\to-1^{+}}\cos(\pi s) = -1, \end{equation*}

it follows that \(\lim_{s\to-1}g(s)\) does not exist. So \(g(s)\) can't be continuous at \(s=-1\text{.}\)

On the other hand, since \(\lim_{s\to1^{-1}}g(s) = 5 = \lim_{s\to1^{+}}g(s)\text{,}\) it follows that \(\lim_{s\to1}g(s)\) exists and is equal to \(5\text{.}\) Since \(g(1)\) also equals \(5\text{,}\) \(g(s)\) is continuous at \(s=1\text{.}\) So \(g(s)\) must be continuous on \((-\infty, -1)\cup(-1,\infty)\text{.}\)
