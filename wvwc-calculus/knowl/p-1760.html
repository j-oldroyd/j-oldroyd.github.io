Suppose we want to find the circulation of a vector field \(\vb{F}\) on a surface \(S\text{.}\) If \(S\) lies in the plane, then we can use Green's Theorem to say that

\begin{equation*} \iint_{S}\curlt{\vb{F}}\,dA = \oint_{\partial S}\vb{F}\cdot\vb{T}\,ds\text{.} \end{equation*}

If \(S\) is a surface in \(\RR^{3}\text{,}\) then we must take the dot product of \(\curlt\vb{F}\) with the unit normal to \(S\text{.}\) The corresponding result is known as Stokes' Theorem.
