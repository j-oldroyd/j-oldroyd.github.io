Solution 9.3.10.1.

We saw previously that

\begin{equation*} \frac{k^{2} + k - 1}{k^{4} + 4k^{2} - 3} \approx \frac{1}{k^{2}}\text{,} \end{equation*}

which suggests comparing the original series with the \(p\)-series \(\sum\frac{1}{k^{2}}\text{.}\) If we let \(a_{k} = \frac{k^{2} + k - 1}{k^{4} + 4k^{2} - 3}\) and \(b_{k} = \frac{1}{k^{2}}\text{,}\) then we see that

\begin{equation*} \frac{a_{k}}{b_{k}} \to 1\text{.} \end{equation*}

By Theorem 9.3.9 and Theorem 9.3.5, the original series converges.
