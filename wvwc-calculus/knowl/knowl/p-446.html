Using all of this, we can define the Riemann sum associated to \(f\) on this partition:

\begin{equation*} \sum_{i=1}^{n}f(x_{i}^{*})\Delta x_{i}. \end{equation*}

Although this can be more complicated than our definition in DefinitionÂ 6.1.1, it still approximates the area under \(f(x)\) from \(x=a\) to \(x=b\text{.}\) And as each subinterval in this partition gets smaller (i.e. as \(\Delta x_{i}\to0\) or equivalently as \(n\to\infty\)), then this approximation should become exact, at least if \(f(x)\) is nice enough.
