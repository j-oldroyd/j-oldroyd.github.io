Now draw a line from the tip of \(\vv{a}\) to the point on \(\vv{b}\) that is closest to the tip of \(\vv{a}\text{;}\) such a line must be orthogonal to \(\vv{b}\text{.}\) This point on \(\vv{b}\) defines a new vector that we call the projection of \(\vv{a}\) onto \(\vv{b}\); we denote this vector by \(\proj{\vv{b}}{\vv{a}}\text{.}\)
