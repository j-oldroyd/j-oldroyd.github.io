An equally useful viewpoint for trigonometric functions is in terms of right triangles. For a right triangle with opposite side subtended by an angle \(\theta\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \cos\theta = \frac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}}\text{ and }\sin\theta = \frac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

This viewpoint is equivalent to the unit circle approach, but in some situations it's easier to work with trigonometric functions using right triangles instead.
