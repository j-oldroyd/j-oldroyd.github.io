We can try dividing the numerator and denominator by the highest power of \(n\) that appears: \(n^{3}\text{.}\) This gives

\begin{equation*} a_{n} = \frac{3n^{-2} - 1}{5 + 2n^{-1}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Now we can take the limit as \(n\to\infty\) to get \(a_{n}\to\frac{-1}{5}\text{.}\)
