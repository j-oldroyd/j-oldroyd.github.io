Example 11.4.2. Cross product of basis vectors.

Let's start by computing \(\mathbf{i}\times\mathbf{k}\) using the definition. If we do so, we have

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{i}\times\mathbf{k} = \dotprod{0,-1,0} = -\mathbf{j}. \end{equation*}

On the other hand, we also have \(\mathbf{k}\times\mathbf{i} = \mathbf{j}\text{.}\) This points out the very important fact that order matters for cross products.
