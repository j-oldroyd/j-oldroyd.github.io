Consider a curve \(\vv{r}(t)\text{.}\) Then the direction of the curve is given by the unit tangent vector \(\vv{T}(t) = \frac{\vv{r}'(t)}{\norm{\vv{r}'(t)}}\text{.}\) If we differentiate \(\vv{T}(t)\) to get \(\vv{T}'(t)\text{,}\) we can view this vector as telling us the direction the curve is turning. This leads us to the concept of unit normal vectors to a space curve.
