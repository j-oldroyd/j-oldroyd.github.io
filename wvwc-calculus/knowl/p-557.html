Recall that

\begin{equation*} \sinh x = \frac{e^{x} - e^{-x}}{2} \quad \text{and} \quad \cosh x = \frac{e^{x} + e^{-x}}{2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

From here we can define hyperbolic trigonometric functions such as \(\tanh x, \sech x, \csch x\) and \(\coth x\text{.}\) It's also easy to show that \(\dv{}{x}\sinh x = \cosh x\) and \(\dv{}{x}\cosh x = \sinh x\text{,}\) and the quotient rule provides derivatives for the other hyperbolic trigonometric functions.
