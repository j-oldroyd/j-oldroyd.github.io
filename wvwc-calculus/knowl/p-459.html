We need to identify the Riemann sum inside the limit as an expression of the form \(\sum f(x_{k}^{*})\Delta x_{k}\text{.}\) We'll guess that \(f(x_{k}^{*}) = \tan\frac{k\pi}{4n}\) and \(\Delta x_{k} = \frac{\pi}{4n}\text{.}\) If we choose \(x_{0} = 0, x_{1} = \frac{\pi}{4n}, x_{2} = \frac{2\pi}{4n},\ldots, x_{n} = \frac{n\pi}{4n}\text{,}\) we get a partition of the interval \([0, \frac{\pi}{4}]\) satisfying \(\Delta x_{k} = \frac{\pi}{4n}\text{.}\) Now we just set \(x_{k}^{*} = \frac{k\pi}{4n}\) (which corresponds to a right Riemann sum). Therefore the limit corresponds to the area under the graph of \(f(x) = \tan x\) over \([0,\frac{\pi}{4}]\text{.}\)
