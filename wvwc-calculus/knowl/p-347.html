The idea of the proof is to use Theorem 4.2.3 to show that \(f(x_{2}) - f(x_{1}) = 0\) for any points \(x_{1},x_{2}\) in \((a,b)\text{.}\) Now, by Theorem 4.2.3 we have

\begin{equation*} f(x_{2}) - f(x_{1}) = f'(c)(x_{2}-x_{1}) \end{equation*}

for some \(c\) in \((a,b)\text{.}\) However, \(f'(c) = 0\) by assumption, and so \(f(x_{2}) - f(x_{1}) = 0\text{.}\) In other words, \(f(x_{2}) = f(x_{1})\text{.}\) Hence \(f\) must be constant.
