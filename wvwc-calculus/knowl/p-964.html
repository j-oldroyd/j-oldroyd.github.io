In SectionÂ 8.6 we found how to obtain power series using the geometric series. What we'll do in this section is determine how to find more general power series. To start, suppose we can represent \(f(x)\) as a power series centered at some number \(a\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} f(x) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}c_{k}(x - a)^{k} = c_{0} + c_{1}(x - a) + c_{2}(x - a)^{2} + c_{3}(x - a)^{3} + \cdots \end{equation*}

Then this representation is valid on some interval \((a - R, a + R)\text{.}\) Our goal is to find what the coefficients \(c_{k}\) are.
