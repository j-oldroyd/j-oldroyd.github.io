Theorem 9.7.1. Taylor's Formula.

If \(f(x)\) has a power series representation/expansion at \(a\) with positive radius of convergence \(R\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} f(x) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}c_{k}(x - a)^{k} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} c_{k} = \frac{f^{(k)}(a)}{k!}\text{.} \end{equation*}

This series is called the Taylor series of \(f\) about \(a\text{.}\)
