The double integral, like the Calculus I integral, derivative and partial derivatives, is linear. This means that you can break it up over addition and pull constants outside of it. In other words, if \(f\) and \(g\) are integrable and if \(c\) is a constant, then

\begin{align*} \iint_{R}(f(x,y) + g(x,y))\,dA & = \iint_{R}f(x,y)\,dA + \iint_{R}g(x,y)\,dA \\ \iint_{R}cf(x,y)\,dA & = c\iint_{R}f(x,y)\,dA \end{align*}
