Any point \(P\) on the circle can be described solely using the distance \(r\) and the direction \(\theta\text{.}\) This leads directly to the idea of polar coordinates. In polar coordinates, the \(xy\)-plane is replaced with an \(r\theta\)-plane, or polar plane. Each point in the polar plane has the form \((r,\theta)\text{,}\) where the radial coordinate \(r\) denotes the distance from the origin, or pole, and the angular coordinate \(\theta\) determines the angle the point makes with the horizontal polar axis.
