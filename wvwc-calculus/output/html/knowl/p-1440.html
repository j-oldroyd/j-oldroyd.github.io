Suppose we have a function \(f(x,y)\) defined at a point \((x_{0},y_{0})\text{.}\) We want to find how quickly \(f(x,y)\) is changing in the direction of some unit vector \(\vb{u} = \dotprod{a,b}\text{.}\) We can estimate this by moving from the point \((x_{0},y_{0})\) by some small increment \(h\vb{u}\text{,}\) and evaluating \(f\) at this new point, which is just \((x_{0} + ha, y_{0} + hb)\text{.}\) So the approximate rate of change in \(f\) in the direction of \(\vb{u}\) is given by

\begin{equation*} \frac{f(x_{0}+ha, y_{0} + hb) - f(x_{0},y_{0})}{h} \end{equation*}

As \(h\to0\text{,}\) this should become exact. This leads us to our next definition.
