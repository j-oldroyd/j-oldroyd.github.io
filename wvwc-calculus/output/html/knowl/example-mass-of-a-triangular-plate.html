Example 13.4.1. Mass of a triangular plate.

Find the mass of the plate contained in the triangular region \(D\) bounded by lines \(y=x, y = 6\) and \(2x+y = 6\text{,}\) given that the density is \(\rho(x,y) = x^{2}\text{.}\)
Solution.
The mass is
\begin{align*} \iint_{D}x^{2}\,A & = \int_{2}^{6}\int_{\frac{6-y}{2}}^{y}x^{2}\,dx\,dy\\ & = \frac{1}{3}\int_{2}^{6}\left[\frac{y^{3} - (6-y)^{3}}{8}\right]\,dy \end{align*}
