Let \(S\) denote a simple, closed, orientable surface with unit normal \(\vb{n}\) and let \(\vb{F}\) denote a continuously differentiable vector field on \(S\text{.}\) Then
\begin{equation*} \oint_{\partial S}\vb{F}\cdot\vb{T}\,ds = \iint_{S}\curl\vb{F}\,d\vb{S}\text{,} \end{equation*}
where \(\partial S\) is oriented counterclockwise with respect to \(\vb{n}\text{.}\)
