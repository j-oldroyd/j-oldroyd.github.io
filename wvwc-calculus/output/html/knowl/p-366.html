Theorem 5.3.1 is an example of an existence theorem. It tells us nothing about how to find the number \(c\text{,}\) or how many such \(c\) can exist. It only tells us that there is at least one number \(c\) in \((a,b)\) for which \(f^{\prime}(c) = 0\text{.}\) This may not seem very useful, but existence theorems can be quite powerful in mathematics.
