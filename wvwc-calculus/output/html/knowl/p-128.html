Let \(f(x)\) be a function. Then its derivative at \(x=a\) is the number \(f'(a)\) given by
\begin{equation*} f'(a) = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h} \end{equation*}
assuming the limit exists. If this limit exists, we say that the function \(f(x)\) is differentiable at \(a\text{.}\)
