The directional derivative of the function \(f(x,y)\) at the point \((x_{0},y_{0})\) in the direction of the vector \(\vb{u} = \dotprod{a,b}\) is given by
\begin{equation*} D_{\vb{u}}f(x_{0},y_{0}) = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x_{0}+ha, y_{0} + hb) - f(x_{0},y_{0})}{h} \end{equation*}
assuming this limit exists. As we mentioned above, \(D_{\vb{i}}f = f_{x}\) and \(D_{\vb{j}}f = f_{y}\text{.}\)
