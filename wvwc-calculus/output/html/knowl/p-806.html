First, note that our solution will be of the form \(x(t)\text{.}\) To find it, we will separate variables and then integrate:
\begin{align*} \dv{x}{t} = 2tx(1-x) \amp \implies \frac{1}{x(1-x)}\dd{x} = 2t\dd{t}\\ \amp \implies \int\frac{\dd{x}}{x(1-x)} = \int 2t\dd{t} \\ \amp \implies \ln(\abs{x}) - \ln(\abs{1-x}) = t^2+C \\ \amp \implies \ln(\frac{x}{1-x}) = t^2+C \text{.} \end{align*}
