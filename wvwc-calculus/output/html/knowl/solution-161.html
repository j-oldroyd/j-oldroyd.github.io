Solution 8.7.7.1.

First, note that our solution will be of the form \(x(t)\text{.}\) To find it, we will separate variables and then integrate:
\begin{align*} \dv{x}{t} = 2tx(1-x) \amp \implies \frac{1}{x(1-x)}\dd{x} = 2t\dd{t}\\ \amp \implies \int\frac{\dd{x}}{x(1-x)} = \int 2t\dd{t} \\ \amp \implies \ln(\abs{x}) - \ln(\abs{1-x}) = t^2+C \\ \amp \implies \ln(\frac{x}{1-x}) = t^2+C \text{.} \end{align*}
At this point we have an implicit solution of our ODE, and we just need to solve for \(x\) to get the explicit solution. We do so by eliminating the natural logarithm and then rearranging:
\begin{align*} \ln(\abs{\frac{x}{1-x}}) = t^2+C \amp \implies \frac{x}{1-x} = C_1e^{t^2} \\ \amp \implies x = C_1e^{t^2}(1-x) \\ \amp \implies x(1 + C_1e^{t^2}) = C_1e^{t^2}\\ \amp \implies x = \frac{C_1e^{t^2}}{1 + C_1e^{t^2}} \text{.} \end{align*}
Note that if this were an IVP, it would be easiest to find the value of the arbitrary constant immediately after integrating.
