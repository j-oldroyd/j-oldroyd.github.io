Proof

We prove this using the definition of the derivative:
\begin{align*} \dv{}{x}[f(x)g(x)] & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)g(x+h) - f(x)g(x)}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)g(x+h) - f(x+h)g(x) + f(x+h)g(x) - f(x)g(x)}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)[g(x+h) - g(x)] + g(x)[f(x+h) - f(x)]}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}f(x+h)\lim_{h\to0}\frac{g(x+h) - g(x)}{h} + g(x)\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \\ & = f(x)g'(x) + g(x)f'(x). \end{align*}
