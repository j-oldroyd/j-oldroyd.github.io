However, it may be the case that some solutions of \(f''(x) = 0\) are not inflection points. As a simple example, consider \(f(x) = x^4\text{.}\) From the graph of this function it's clearly concave up everywhere even though the \(f''(0) = 0\text{.}\) The problem here is that \(f''(x) = 12x^2\) doesn't change sign at \(x = 0\text{,}\) and so this point can't be an inflection point.
