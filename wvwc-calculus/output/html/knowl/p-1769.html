As an example of a surface that is not orientable, consider the surface parameterized by

\begin{equation*} \vb{r}(u,v) = \dotprod{2\cos u + v\cos\frac{u}{2}, 2\sin u + v\cos\frac{u}{2}, v\sin\frac{u}{2}} \end{equation*}

for \(0\leq u\leq 2\pi\) and \(-\frac{1}{2}\leq v\leq \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) After a lot of algebra, we find that the normal vector \(\vb{r}_{u}\times\vb{r}_{v}\) should be

\begin{equation*} (\frac{v}{2} - 2 \sin{\left (\frac{u}{2} \right )} \cos{\left (u \right )})\vb{i} + (- \frac{v}{2} - 2 \sin{\left (\frac{u}{2} \right )} \sin{\left (u \right )})\vb{j} + (2 \sqrt{2} \sin{\left (u + \frac{\pi}{4} \right )} \cos{\left (\frac{u}{2} \right )})\vb{k}\text{.} \end{equation*}
