Now clear fractions to get
\begin{equation*} 2 = A_{1}y(y-1) + A_{2}(y-1) + A_{3}y^{2}. \end{equation*}
Setting \(y = 0\) gives \(A_{2} = -2\text{,}\) and setting \(y = 1\) gives \(A_{3} = 2\text{.}\) To find \(A_{1}\text{,}\) we can expand the right hand side to write
\begin{equation*} 2 = A_{1}y^{2} - A_{1}y + A_{2}y - A_{2} + A_{3}y^{2}. \end{equation*}
Hence \(A_{1} = -A_{3} = -2\text{.}\)
