Recall that from Calc I, we know that

\begin{equation*} \dv{}{x}f(g(x)) = \dv{f}{g}\dv{g}{x} = f'(g(x))g'(x). \end{equation*}

This is just the the chain rule, which tells us how to differentiate functions that depend on other functions. Or to think of it another way, it tells us how to differentiate variables like \(f\) that depend on other variables like \(g\) and \(x\text{.}\) What we'd like to do now is to extend the chain rule to more complicated situations.
