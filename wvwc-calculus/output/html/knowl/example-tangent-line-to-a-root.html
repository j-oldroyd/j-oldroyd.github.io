Example 3.1.3. Tangent line to a root.

Find the equation of the tangent line to \(y=\sqrt{x}\) through the point \((4,2)\text{.}\)
Solution.
We need two things to find the equation of a line: the slope of the line and a point on the line. Since we know the tangent line has to pass through \((4,2)\text{,}\) we just need to find the slope. The slope is given by
\begin{equation*} \lim_{x\to 4}\frac{\sqrt{x}-2}{x-4} = \lim_{x\to4}\frac{x-4}{(x-4)(\sqrt{x}+2)} = \frac{1}{4}. \end{equation*}
Hence the equation of the tangent line through \((4,2)\) is
\begin{equation*} y-2 = \frac{1}{4}(x-4). \end{equation*}
