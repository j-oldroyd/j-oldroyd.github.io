Example 14.9.2. Flux across a spherical cap.

Let \(S\) denote the top half of the sphere of radius \(5\) centered at the origin. Let \(\vb{F} = \dotprod{x^{3} - y^{3}, y^{3} - z^{3}, z^{3} - x^{3}}\text{.}\) Determine if \(\vb{F}\) flows out of or into \(S\text{.}\)
Solution.
We need to calculate the flux integral \(\iint_{S}\vb{F}\cdot d\vb{S}\text{,}\) which is made easier using TheoremÂ 14.9.1. If we let \(E\) denote the interior of this region, we get
\begin{align*} \iint_{S}\vb{F}\cdot d\vb{S} \amp \iiint_{E}\div\vb{F}\,dV \\ \amp = \iiint_{E}3(x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2})\,dV \\ \amp = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}\int_{0}^{5}3\rho^{4}\sin\varphi\,d\rho\,d\varphi\,d\theta \end{align*}
