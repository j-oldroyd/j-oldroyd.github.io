You may think that a continuous function must at least be differentiable "almost everywhere" at this point. After all, how could it be possible to draw a graph without lifting your pencil off the paper that still has a cusp or a vertical tangent line everywhere? Most mathematicians before the \(19^{\text{th}}\) century thought this as well, until Weierstrass came up with a function, the Weierstrass function 1 , that is continuous everywhere but differentiable nowhere.
