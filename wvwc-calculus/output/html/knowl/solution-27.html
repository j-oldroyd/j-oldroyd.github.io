Solution 3.2.8.1.

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity, and velocity is the rate of change of position. So we should probably find the velocity first! Letâ€™s call it \(v(t)\text{.}\) We have
\begin{align*} v(t) & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{s(t+h) - s(t)}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{3(t+h) - 5(t+h)^{3} - 3t + 5t^{3}}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{3h + 5[t^{3} - (t+h)^{3}]}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{3h + 5[(-h)(t^{2} + t(t+h) + (t+h)^{2})]}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}(3 - 5[t^{2} + t(t+h) + (t+h)^{2}]) \\ & = 3 - 15t^{2} \end{align*}
Now we can get the acceleration as well:
\begin{equation*} a(t) = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{v(t+h) - v(t)}{h} = -30t. \end{equation*}
