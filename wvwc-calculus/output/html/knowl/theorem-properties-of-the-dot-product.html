Theorem 11.3.4. Properties of the Dot Product.

Let \(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}\) and \(\mathbf{z}\) be vectors, and let \(\alpha\) be a scalar. Then the following properties hold:

  1. \(\displaystyle \mathbf{x}\cdot\mathbf{x} = \norm{\mathbf{x}}^{2}.\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \mathbf{x}\cdot\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y}\cdot\mathbf{x}.\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \mathbf{x}\cdot(\mathbf{y}+\mathbf{z}) = \mathbf{x}\cdot\mathbf{y}+\mathbf{x}\cdot\mathbf{z}.\)

  4. \(\displaystyle (\alpha\mathbf{x})\cdot\mathbf{y} = \alpha(\mathbf{x}\cdot\mathbf{y}) = \mathbf{x}\cdot(\alpha\mathbf{y}).\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \mathbf{0}\cdot\mathbf{x} = 0.\)
