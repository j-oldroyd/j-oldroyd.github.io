Finding magnitudes of vectors can also be done by applying the distance formula from SectionÂ 11.1 to the components of the vectors. The magnitude of a vector \(\mathbf{x}\) is denoted by \(|\mathbf{x}|\) or \(\norm{\mathbf{x}}\text{.}\) For example, the magnitude of \(\mathbf{b}-3\mathbf{a}\) from the previous example is

\begin{equation*} \norm{\mathbf{b}-3\mathbf{a}} = \sqrt{36+1+64} = \sqrt{101}. \end{equation*}
