Solution 7.4.10.1.

This integral is improper since \(\ln x\) is discontinuous at \(x = 0\text{.}\) To evaluate it, we’ll use limits:
\begin{align*} \int_{0}^{1}\ln x\,dx & = \lim_{c\to0^{+}}\int_{c}^{1}\ln x\,dx \\ & = \lim_{c\to0^{+}}\left[x\ln x - x\right]_{c}^{1} \\ & = -1 - \lim_{c\to0^{+}}c\ln c \\ & = -1, \end{align*}
where we used the fact that \(c\ln c\to 0\) as \(c\to0\) by L’Hopital’s Rule.
