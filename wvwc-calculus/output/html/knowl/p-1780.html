In addition to giving us a normal vector to the surface \(S\text{,}\) the quantity \(\vb{r}_{u}\times\vb{r}_{v}\) can also be used to estimate the area of a small “parallelogram” on \(S\) at a given point \(\vb{r}(u_{0}, v_{0})\text{.}\) In particular, if
\begin{align*} u_{0} \amp \leq u\leq u_{0} + \Delta u \\ v_{0} \amp \leq v\leq v_{0} + \Delta v \text{,} \end{align*}
then the area of the surface
\begin{equation*} \vb{r}(u,v) \end{equation*}
subject to the above restrictions on \(u\) and \(v\) is approximately
\begin{equation*} \norm{\vb{r}_{u}\times\vb{r}_{v}}\Delta u\Delta v\text{,} \end{equation*}
