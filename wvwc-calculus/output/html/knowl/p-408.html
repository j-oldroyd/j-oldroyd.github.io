Newton’s Method begins as follows: pick some starting point, or guess, \(x_{1}\text{.}\) Then draw the tangent line \(L\) to the graph of \(f(x)\) at \(x_{1}\text{.}\) Now, the \(x\)-intercept of this line may not be a root, but hopefully it’ll be closer to the root. So call this point \(x_{2}\text{.}\) We can solve for \(x_{2}\) (if \(f^{\prime}(x_{1})\neq0\)) to get
\begin{equation*} x_{2} = x_{1} - \frac{f(x_{1})}{f^{\prime}(x_{1})}. \end{equation*}
Now, at this point \(x_{2}\) is (hopefully!) a better approximation to the root \(x_{0}\) than \(x_{1}\text{,}\) and we can run through the same procedure to get a new point \(x_{3}\text{.}\)
