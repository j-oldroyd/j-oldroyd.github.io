Definition 6.3.5. Indefinite Integral.

Let \(f(x)\) be a continuous function. The indefinite integral of \(f(x)\text{,}\) denoted \(\int f(x)\,dx\text{,}\) is the general antiderivative of \(f(x)\text{.}\) In symbols, if \(F(x)\) is an antiderivative of \(f(x)\) then
\begin{equation*} \int f(x)\,dx = F(x) + C. \end{equation*}
