We say that a function is harmonic if its Laplacian is \(0\text{.}\) The equation \(\Delta f = 0\) is known as Laplace’s equation. Harmonic functions are extremely useful, as they represent quantities in a kind of equilibrium state. If \(f\) represents chemical concentration, temperature or electrostatic potential, then Laplace’s equation is Fick’s law of diffusion, Fourier’s law of heat conduction or Ohm’s law of electrical conduction, respectively.
