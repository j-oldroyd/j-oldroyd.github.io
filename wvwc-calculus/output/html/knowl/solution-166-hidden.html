Let \(P(t)\) denote the population of the USA (in millions), where \(t\) is the number of years after 2010. Then
\begin{equation*} \dv{P}{t} = kP(M-P) \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} P(t) = \frac{MP_{0}}{P_{0} + (M - P_{0})e^{-kMt}}. \end{equation*}
So we need to find \(k\) and \(M\text{.}\)
When \(t = 0\text{,}\) we have \(P^\prime = 2.3\) and \(P = 309.3\text{.}\) Similarly, when \(t = 10\) we have \(P^\prime = 1.2\) and \(P = 329.5\text{.}\) Therefore
\begin{align*} 2.3 \amp = 309.3k(M - 309.3) \\ 1.2 \amp = 329.5k(M - 329.5) \text{.} \end{align*}
