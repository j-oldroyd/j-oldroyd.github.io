Example 13.5.6. Volume using triple integrals.

Find the volume of the region bounded by the cylinder \(y = x^{2}\) and the planes \(z = 3-y\) and \(z=0\text{.}\)
Solution.
If we let \(D\) denote this region, then its volume is given by \(\iiint_{D}\,dV\text{.}\) The volume is then
\begin{align*} \iiint_{D}\,dV & = \int_{-\sqrt{3}}^{\sqrt{3}}\int_{x^{2}}^{3}\int_{0}^{3-y}\,dz\,dy\,dx \\ & = \int_{-\sqrt{3}}^{\sqrt{3}}\int_{x^{2}}^{3} (3-y)\,dy\,dx \\ & = \int_{-\sqrt{3}}^{\sqrt{3}}\left[3y-\frac{y^{2}}{2}\right]_{y=x^{2}}^{3}\,dx \\ & = \int_{-\sqrt{3}}^{\sqrt{3}}\left[9 - \frac{9}{2} - 3x^{2} + \frac{x^{2}}{2}\right]\,dx \\ & = \frac{24\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{align*}
