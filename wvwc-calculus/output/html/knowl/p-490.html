Let \(f(x)\) be a continuous function. Then
\begin{equation*} \int_{a}^{b}f(x)\,dx \approx M_{n} = \sum_{k=1}^{n}f(x_{k}^{*})\Delta x \end{equation*}
where
\begin{align*} x_{k}^{*} & = \frac{x_{k-1}+x_{k}}{2} \\ \Delta x & = \frac{b-a}{n} \text{.} \end{align*}
Note that this rule calls for evenly dividing \([a,b]\) into a partition \([x_{k-1},x_{k}]\) where each subinterval has equal length \(\Delta x\text{.}\)
