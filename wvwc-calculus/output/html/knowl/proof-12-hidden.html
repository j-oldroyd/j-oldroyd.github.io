By the product rule, we have \(\dv{}{x}[f(x)g(x)] = f(x)g^{\prime}(x) + f^{\prime}(x)g(x)\text{.}\) Rearranging this, we have
\begin{equation*} f(x)g^{\prime}(x) = [f(x)g(x)]^{\prime} - f^{\prime}(x)g(x). \end{equation*}
Finally, we can integrate both sides to get
\begin{equation*} \int f(x)g^{\prime}(x)\,dx = f(x)g(x) - \int g(x)f^{\prime}(x)\,dx. \end{equation*}
