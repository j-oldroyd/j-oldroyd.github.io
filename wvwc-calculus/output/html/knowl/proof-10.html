Proof

First, partition \([a,b]\) into \([x_{0},x_{1}],\ldots,[x_{n-1},x_{n}]\text{.}\) Then we can say that
\begin{align*} F(b) - F(a) & = F(x_{n}) - F(x_{0}) \\ & = F(x_{n}) - F(x_{n-1}) + F(x_{n-1}) - F(x_{n-2}) + \cdots + F(x_{1}) - F(x_{0}) \\ & = \sum_{i=1}^{n}[F(x_{i}) - F(x_{i-1})]. \end{align*}
By the Mean Value Theorem, we can rewrite each term in the sum using \(F^{\prime}\text{:}\)
\begin{equation*} F(x_{i}) - F(x_{i-1}) = F^{\prime}(x_{i}^{*})(x_{i} - x_{i-1}) \end{equation*}
where \(x_{i}^{*}\) is just some point in \([x_{i-1},x_{i}]\text{.}\) So we have
\begin{align*} F(b) - F(a) & = \sum_{i=1}^{n}F^{\prime}(x_{i}^{*})(x_{i} - x_{i-1}) \\ & = \sum_{i=1}^{n}f(x_{i}^{*})\Delta x. \end{align*}
If we now send \(n\to\infty\) (i.e,, \(\Delta x\to 0\)), then the above becomes
\begin{equation*} F(b) - F(a) = \int_{a}^{b}f(x)\,dx. \end{equation*}
