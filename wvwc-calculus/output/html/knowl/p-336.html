Example 5.1.6 shows that \(0\cdot\infty\) indeterminate forms can be dealt with by rewriting them in the form \(\frac{0}{0}\) or \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\) and then applying L’Hospital’s Rule. It’s trickier, but we can also deal with \(\infty-\infty\) indeterminate forms by rewriting them in this way.
