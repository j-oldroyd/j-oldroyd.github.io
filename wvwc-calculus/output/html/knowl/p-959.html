We’ll prove the contrapositive of this statement. That is, we’ll show that if the series converges then the terms go to \(0\text{.}\) So suppose \(\sum a_{k}\) converges and let \(S_{n}\) denote the sequence of partial sums. Then \(a_{n+1} = S_{n+1} - S_{n}\) which must go to \(0\) since the partial sums converge.
