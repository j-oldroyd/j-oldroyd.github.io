By definition, the average value of \(f\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{\operatorname{vol}(S^{2})}\iiint_{S^{2}}f(x,y,z)\,dV\text{.} \end{equation*}
We’ll follow the same strategy we used in Example 13.7.2 to compute this integral. If we convert to spherical coordinates, we get
\begin{align*} \frac{3}{4\pi}\int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{\pi}\int_{0}^{1}\rho^{2}e^{-\rho^{3}}\sin\varphi \,d\rho\,d\varphi\,d\theta \amp = \frac{3}{2}\int_{0}^{\pi}\int_{0}^{1}\rho^{2}e^{-\rho^{3}}\sin\varphi \,d\rho\,d\varphi \\ \amp = \frac{1}{2}\int_{0}^{\pi}\int_{-1}^{0}e^{u}\sin\varphi \,du\,d\varphi \\ \amp = 1 - \frac{1}{e} \text{.} \end{align*}
