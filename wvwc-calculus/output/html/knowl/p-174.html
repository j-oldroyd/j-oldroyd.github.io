Many functions can be written in the form \(f(x)g(x)\text{,}\) where \(f(x),g(x)\) may each have previously known derivatives. What we want to do now is to find a way to get the derivative of \(f(x)g(x)\) from the derivatives of \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\text{.}\) We do this using the product rule.
