Example 10.2.1. Cusps on a Cycloid.

The parametric equations
\begin{align*} x \amp = t - \sin t \\ y \amp = 1 - \cos t \end{align*}
trace out a cycloid, also known as the brachistochrone or curve of fastest descent. Find the values of \(t\) for which this curve has a cusp.
Solution.
First, note that a curve cannot be differentiable at a cusp. So we can try to find the cusps by looking at where \(\dv{y}{x}\) fails to exist. This occurs precisely at \(t = 2k\pi\) for any integer \(k\text{.}\) Hence the cycloid should have a cusp at these values of \(t\text{.}\)
