We can also find higher partial derivatives. For example, the second partial derivatives of a function \(f(x,y)\) are given by

\begin{align*} \pdv[2]{f}{x} & = \pdv{}{x}\left(\pdv{f}{x}\right) = (f_{x})_{x} \\ \pdv[2]{f}{y} & = \pdv{}{y}\left(\pdv{f}{y}\right) = (f_{y})_{y} \\ \pdv{{}^{2}f}{x\partial y} & = \pdv{}{x}\left(\pdv{f}{y}\right) = (f_{y})_{x} \\ \pdv{{}^{2}f}{y\partial x} & = \pdv{}{y}\left(\pdv{f}{x}\right) = (f_{x})_{y} \end{align*}

The last two derivatives are examples of mixed derivatives.
