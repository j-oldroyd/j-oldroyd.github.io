Example 6.3.2. Modeling a population.

A honeybee population starts with \(100\) members and grows at a rate of \(n^{\prime}(t)\) per week. Find the total population after \(15\) weeks in terms of \(n^{\prime}(t)\text{.}\)
Solution.
The total population after \(15 weeks\) should be given by
\begin{align*} (\text{total population}) & = (\text{initial population}) + (\text{net change}) \\ & = 100 + \int_{0}^{15}n^{\prime}(t)\,dt \end{align*}
