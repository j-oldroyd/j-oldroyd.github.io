Section 3.4 Product and Quotient Rules

Subsection 3.4.1 Product Rule

Many functions can be written in the form \(f(x)g(x)\text{,}\) where \(f(x),g(x)\) may each have previously known derivatives. What we want to do now is to find a way to get the derivative of \(f(x)g(x)\) from the derivatives of \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\text{.}\) We do this using the product rule.

Theorem 3.4.1. The Product Rule.

Let \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) be differentiable functions. Then

\begin{equation*} \dv{}{x}[f(x)g(x)] = f(x)g'(x) + f'(x)g(x). \end{equation*}

Proof.

We prove this using the definition of the derivative:

\begin{align*} \dv{}{x}[f(x)g(x)] & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)g(x+h) - f(x)g(x)}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)g(x+h) - f(x+h)g(x) + f(x+h)g(x) - f(x)g(x)}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h)[g(x+h) - g(x)] + g(x)[f(x+h) - f(x)]}{h} \\ & = \lim_{h\to0}f(x+h)\lim_{h\to0}\frac{g(x+h) - g(x)}{h} + g(x)\lim_{h\to0}\frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \\ & = f(x)g'(x) + g(x)f'(x). \end{align*}

Example 3.4.2. Using the product rule.

Let \(J(v) = (v^{10} - 2\sin v)(\cos v + \frac{1}{\sqrt[3]{x^{4}}})\text{.}\) Find \(J'(v)\text{.}\)

Solution.

We could foil this out and take derivatives, but it will be easier to use the product rule.

Subsection 3.4.2 The Quotient Rule

Now that we know how to differentiate products, we move on to quotients.

Theorem 3.4.3. The Quotient Rule.

Let \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) be differentiable functions. Then

\begin{equation*} \dv{}{x}\left[\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right] = \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^{2}} \end{equation*}

wherever \(g(x)\neq0\text{.}\)

Example 3.4.4. Derivative of tangent.

Let \(f(x) = \tan x\text{.}\) Find \(f'(x)\text{.}\)

Solution.

Since \(\tan x = \frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\text{,}\) then we can apply the quotient rule to get the derivative of \(\tan x\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \dv{}{x}\tan x = \frac{\cos^{2}x + \sin^{2}x}{\cos^{2}x} = \sec^{2}x. \end{equation*}

The derivatives for \(\sec x, \csc x\) and \(\cot x\) may also be computed using the quotient rule and the facts that \(\dv{}{x}\sin x = \cos x\) and \(\dv{}{x}\cos x = -\sin x\text{.}\)
