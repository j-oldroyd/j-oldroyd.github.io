Section 14.9 The Divergence Theorem

So far we've considered several different versions of the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus. There is the Fundamental Theorem of Line Integrals which applies to the gradient operator \(\grad\text{,}\) Green's Theorem which applies to the two-dimensional curl, Stokes' Theorem which applies to \(\curl\text{,}\) and also Green's Theorem for Flux Integrals which applies to the two-dimensional divergence. The only remaining generalization is the divergence theorem.

Theorem 14.9.1. Divergence Theorem.

Let \(E\) be a simple solid region and let \(S\) be the boundary surface of \(E\) oriented outwards. Let \(\vb{F}\) denote a continuously differentiable vector field on \(E\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \iint_{S}\vb{F}\cdot d\vb{S} = \iiint_{E}\div\vb{F}\,dV\text{.} \end{equation*}
Example 14.9.2. Flux across a spherical cap.

Let \(S\) denote the top half of the sphere of radius \(5\) centered at the origin. Let \(\vb{F} = \dotprod{x^{3} - y^{3}, y^{3} - z^{3}, z^{3} - x^{3}}\text{.}\) Determine if \(\vb{F}\) flows out of or into \(S\text{.}\)

Solution

We need to calculate the flux integral \(\iint_{S}\vb{F}\cdot d\vb{S}\text{,}\) which is made easier using TheoremÂ 14.9.1. If we let \(E\) denote the interior of this region, we get

\begin{align*} \iint_{S}\vb{F}\cdot d\vb{S} \amp \iiint_{E}\div\vb{F}\,dV \\ \amp = \iiint_{E}3(x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2})\,dV \\ \amp = \int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{\frac{\pi}{2}}\int_{0}^{5}3\rho^{4}\sin\varphi\,d\rho\,d\varphi\,d\theta \end{align*}
Example 14.9.3. Flux across a cube.

Let \(S\) denote the surface of the unit cube with vertices at \((0,0,0)\) and \((1,1,1)\) and let \(\vb{F} = \dotprod{xy, y^{2}z, z^{3}}\text{.}\) Find \(\iint_{S}\vb{F}\cdot\vb{n}\,dS\) where \(\vb{n}\) is the inward unit normal to \(S\text{.}\)
